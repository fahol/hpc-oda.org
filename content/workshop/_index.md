      title: 'HPC ODA Workshop at SC26'
      subtitle: ''
      text: |-
        High-performance computing (HPC) systems generate vast streams of operational data: system logs, job scheduler records, hardware metrics, energy consumption, network performance, and environmental telemetry. This data is a foundational resource for the entire HPC ecosystem. Operations teams rely on it for system reliability, efficiency, and predictable maintenance. Researchers need it for training AI/ML models, constructing digital twins, and studying system behavior at scale. Users need it to understand application behavior, diagnose performance bottlenecks, and optimize their resource utilization. Yet, access to operational data and the capability to analyze it remains unevenly distributed across these communities, creating a gap between the recognized value of the data and its practical utilization. Operational data analytics (ODA) addresses this gap through methods to collect, integrate, process, and share operational data across disciplines at various timescales. As HPC converges with AI-driven workloads and autonomous operations, operational data is moving from a specialized operations concern to a central topic in HPC research and practice, requiring innovative approaches in cross-community data integration, scalable analytics, and real-time processing.
